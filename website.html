The Attica Prison Riot

What was the Attica Prison Riot?

Attica Prison is a prison in Attica, New York. This is a maximum security prison. At the time 2,200 men were incarcerated there at the time of the riot. The conditions in the prison are what caused the riot. The riot happened in 1971. The riot lasted 4 days from September 9th - September 13th. This is one of the largest prison riots of all time. It is still taught and used as a lesson today.

Why did the inmates Riot?

The main reason the inmates rioted was the very poor conditions. Inmates inside the prison talked about how dirty and disgusting that it was. When they entered the prison inmates entered the prison in shackles and leg irons. Inmates when they got their when right into isolation for 2 days. This was meant to set a bad president from the moment that the inmates arrived at the prison. They were also put into gray prison uniforms which they could not take off.

Inside the prison

The cells were very small. They were only the size of a closet. That comes out to being 6 feet by 9 feet. Which is way to small for people to be living in. Also the cells did not have a window so there was no natural light. The closest window was across the hall. They have a bed, a stool, a table and a toilet. Which is not much at all when you think about how they are there all the time. The toilet was open and how no cover so it smelt very bad all the time.
